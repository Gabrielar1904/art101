Hi everyone, am I a first year at UCSC and have always loved art. Ever since I was a kid, I would enjoy crafting as much as possible with anything I could find. Over the years, I began to take art classes to explore different art mediums. I felt like the classes were nice to know which mediums worked for me and which ones didn't. Then high school began and I started to lose touch with my artistc side. During the pandemic, I realized that making art was a great emotional outlet for me and I began to dabble in line art to break away from the conventional forms of art that made me feel like I had to be a perfectionist and stay within the lines. I like getting to know different forms of art and that is why I chose this course. I knew that it would be a challenge since there is a lot of coding involved and because I have absolutely no experience with coding. It is something that will test my limits in a new way, which I am intrested to see turn out. In regards to my major, sociology is another passionate of mine. Coming from a Latinx background and being a first - gen, I have always been curious about the institutions and social structures that have impacted me as an individual living in America. I also am very passionate about social justice and love learning about the root cause of current systemic discriminations that minorities experience.
